⚡ CBAM Definitive Regime Begins

The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism enters full implementation on January 1, 2026, requiring importers to purchase certificates for embedded emissions in carbon-intensive goods. The expanded scope now covers 180 new downstream products including machinery and appliances.

Country Spotlights

🇳🇱 Netherlands

The Netherlands continues to lead high-tech circularity with its National Circular Economy Programme 2023-2030 targeting priority product groups including electronics, packaging, and textiles. Key initiatives include:

  • Digital product passports for full lifecycle tracking
  • Philips medical device refurbishment reducing 40% of equipment waste
  • AI-enabled sorting of aluminum scrap improving recovery rates
  • Events like "Circularity in Electronics" bringing together industry and policymakers

🇬🇧 United Kingdom

The UK Energy Technology List (ETL) now includes over 8,000 independently verified high-efficiency products across 60+ categories. Recent additions include the NORD Gear 0.55 kW IE4 motor (September 2025), supporting:

  • Retrofit programes for businesses upgrading to low-energy equipment
  • Top 25% energy-efficient product standards
  • Categories: boiler retrofit kits, lighting, motors, refrigeration, HVAC, UPS
  • Alignment with net-zero product policy

🇪🇸 Spain

España Circular 2030 strategy advances with EU-funded projects targeting 30% reduction in material consumption relative to GDP, 15% less waste generation, and halving per-capita food waste by 2030. Highlights include:

  • CLIC RECYCLE cross-border project (Spain, Portugal, Netherlands) converting salon hair waste into biodegradable coastal cleaning products
  • Circular textiles and industrial process projects
  • Community engagement models for circular business adoption

🇵🇹 Portugal

Portugal launched new measures in December 2025 to accelerate circular business models across priority sectors, supported by Portugal Global and national funding programes promoting sustainable procurement and circular product design.

CLIC RECYCLE - Cross-Border Circular Innovation

Spain, Portugal, Netherlands | Sustainable Materials

CLIC RECYCLE collects hair waste from over 200 salons across Spain, Portugal, and the Netherlands, transforming it into biodegradable products that clean coastal waters and regenerate soils.
