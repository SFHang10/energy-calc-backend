📚 HVAC & Energy Glossary

Key terms and concepts for understanding commercial HVAC energy efficiency.

HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning)

Building systems that control temperature, humidity, and air quality. In commercial buildings, HVAC typically accounts for 40-60% of total energy consumption.

HRV (Heat Recovery Ventilation)

Ventilation system that recovers heat from exhaust air and transfers it to incoming fresh air, reducing heating/cooling loads. Typical efficiency ranges from 60-85% depending on design and conditions.

ETL (Energy Technology List)

UK government database of energy-efficient products meeting strict performance criteria. ETL-listed equipment qualifies for Enhanced Capital Allowances (100% first-year tax relief) and demonstrates verified energy savings.

Visit ETL Database →

EC Motor (Electronically Commutated)

High-efficiency brushless DC motor with integrated electronics. EC motors are 30-40% more efficient than standard AC motors and enable precise variable-speed control, making them ideal for HVAC fans.

Specific Fan Power (SFP)

Energy efficiency metric for ventilation systems, measured in watts per liter per second [W/(l/s)]. Lower SFP indicates better efficiency. ETL HRV systems typically achieve 0.45-0.65 W/(l/s) vs 1.0-1.5 W/(l/s) for older systems.

AHU (Air Handling Unit)

Central HVAC component that conditions and distributes air throughout a building. Includes fans, filters, heating/cooling coils, and optionally heat recovery. AHU fan energy represents ~46% of total HVAC electricity in typical buildings.

kWh (Kilowatt-Hour)

Unit of energy consumption equal to 1,000 watts used for one hour. Your electricity bill is calculated from kWh usage. A typical 1,000 m² commercial building uses 440,000-460,000 kWh annually for HVAC.

Year-over-Year (YoY) Growth

Percentage change in a metric (energy, cost) compared to the same period in the previous year. HVAC energy typically grows 1-4% YoY from weather variations and equipment degradation; ETL HRV reduces consumption -18% to -23% YoY.

Variable Speed Drive (VSD)

Electronic system that controls motor speed by varying frequency and voltage. VSDs enable fans and pumps to match actual demand rather than running at fixed speeds, saving 20-50% energy during partial load conditions (which occur 80%+ of operating hours).

Heat Exchanger

Device that transfers thermal energy between two fluid streams without mixing them. In HRV systems, crossflow or counter-flow heat exchangers recover 75-85% of heat from exhaust air to preheat incoming fresh air in winter (or pre-cool in summer).

CO₂ Emissions Factor

Amount of CO₂ released per unit of energy generated. UK grid electricity produces approximately 0.5 kg CO₂ per kWh. Reducing HVAC energy by 63,000 kWh/year prevents 31,500 kg (31.5 tonnes) of CO₂ emissions annually.

Simple Payback Period

Time required for energy savings to equal the additional upfront cost of efficient equipment. For ETL HRV, the additional £50,000 investment saves £17,010/year, yielding a 2.9-year simple payback (before tax benefits or grants).
