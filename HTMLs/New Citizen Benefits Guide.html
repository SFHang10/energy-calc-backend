Insulation

Up to €3,000 grant

Cavity wall and loft insulation. Reduces heating bills by 20-30%. Fully funded for low-income households in many regions.

What this means for you: Insulation support is usually offered as a grant or voucher once a basic home assessment confirms eligibility.

  • Typical measures: loft, cavity wall, and draught-proofing.
  • Often prioritizes homes with high heating costs or low EPC ratings.
  • Expect 1–2 contractor quotes and a short approval window.

What to prepare: address, recent energy bill, and (if available) EPC rating.

🪟

Double Glazing

Up to €2,500 grant

Replace single-pane windows. Save €150-300/year on heating. 0% interest loans available in Netherlands and UK.

🪟 Example savings breakdown (typical home):

Before: €1,200/yr heating
After: €900–1,050/yr
Savings: €150–300/yr
Comfort: fewer drafts + quieter

Savings vary by window type, home size, and climate. Grants/0% loans can cut payback time significantly.

⚡

Smart Thermostats

€50-150 rebate

Free installation with some energy suppliers. Automatically optimize heating for 10-15% savings.

What you get: A smart thermostat that learns your routine and avoids heating empty rooms.

  • Typical savings: 10–15% on heating bills.
  • Works best with gas boilers or heat pumps.
  • Most installs take under 1 hour.

How it helps: schedules + occupancy sensing + weather adjustments reduce wasted heat.

🔧 Repair, Reuse & Circular Economy Benefits

New EU "Right to Repair" laws and circular economy initiatives give you:

🔧

Repair Subsidies

50% off repairs

How it works: Take your broken appliance (TV, washing machine, phone) to a certified repair shop. Government pays half the cost.

Available in:
• Spain (various cities)
• Netherlands (pilot programs)
• Austria, France (national programs)
♻️

Trade-In Programs

€50-200 value

What you get: Bring old electronics to retailers. Get credit toward new efficient models. Your old device is properly recycled.

Accepted items:
• Phones, tablets, laptops
• Small appliances
• Batteries, chargers
🎁

Extended Warranties

Free

New EU rules: Products must be repairable for 7-10 years. Manufacturers must stock spare parts. You get guaranteed repairs at fair prices.

Applies to:
• Appliances (from 2026)
• Electronics
• Furniture
⚠️ Don't Throw It Away! From 2026, retailers must accept your old electronics for recycling when you buy new. You can also get free collection from many councils.
