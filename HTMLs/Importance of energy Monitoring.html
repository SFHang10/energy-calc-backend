Why Measure Energy Data?

Measuring energy consumption reveals hidden waste, identifies inefficiencies, and empowers targeted action. From restaurants cutting refrigeration costs to offices optimizing HVAC schedules, data-driven decisions deliver measurable financial and environmental returns.

🏠 Homes

Submetering and consumption-based billing typically leads to 10-15% lower energy use. Residents clearly see their own consumption patterns and can make informed decisions about heating, lighting, and appliance usage.

🏢 Offices

Automatic monitoring and targeting (aM&T) systems help businesses identify 4-20% energy savings with average cost reductions around 10-15% by revealing patterns, anomalies, and unnecessary baseloads.

🍽️ Restaurants

Granular monitoring of refrigeration, HVAC, and cooking equipment highlights abnormal loads and out-of-hours use, enabling staff to quickly intervene and reduce waste while maintaining service quality.

Monitoring Methods

From whole-building meters to IoT sensors, multiple approaches enable comprehensive energy visibility

1. Whole-Building Metering

Main utility meters with interval logging (15-minute data) track total consumption and trends. Ideal for high-level tracking, bill validation, and before/after comparisons.

2. Sub-metering & aM&T

Submeters on key circuits enable load disaggregation by zone or function. Automatic collection to software platforms provides analysis, alerts, and actionable insights.

3. Portable Monitoring

Temporary clamp-on analyzers and data loggers measure power and quality parameters. Perfect for audits, troubleshooting, and validating savings without permanent installation.

4. IoT & BMS Integration

Smart devices and building management systems combine energy data with occupancy, temperature, and equipment status for advanced analytics and automated optimization.

5. Tenant Metering

Individual meters in multi-unit buildings enable consumption-based billing. Transparency drives 10-15% average savings across EU housing stock when combined with regular feedback.

6. Digital Optimization

Energy analytics platforms benchmark performance, detect anomalies, and continuously optimize systems. European buildings achieve 20-40% heating savings using data-driven optimization.

European Success Stories

Real-world examples of energy monitoring delivering substantial savings across Europe

Amsterdam & Bilbao

Netherlands & Spain - ATELIER Project

Through the ATELIER project, parts of Amsterdam and Bilbao are being transformed into "Positive Energy Districts" where districts generate more energy than they consume. Detailed monitoring of building consumption, local generation (solar), and storage optimizes self-consumption and proves that neighbourhood-scale decarbonisation is both technically and economically viable.

Official source: ATELIER ↗

E.ON Glasgow Office

United Kingdom

Smart thermostatic radiator valve devices on two office floors with 5-minute data collection enabled room-by-room scheduling aligned with actual occupancy. Within two months, heating energy use dropped by 33%, saving 4.2 MWh and avoiding 0.8 tonnes of CO₂ while better matching comfort to hybrid working patterns.

Official source: EcoSync case study ↗

European Hotel Project

DoubleTree by Hilton Dartford Bridge, UK

A European hotel combining an energy centre, CHP units, and comprehensive energy monitoring reduced grid electricity dependence by 75% and achieved total energy savings of 65%, translating to cost reductions of approximately £377,000 in 12 months.

Official source: Spacewell case study ↗

Digital Optimization in Germany, Denmark & Spain

Multiple EU Countries - WE Data Europe

Real-life projects using submetering and smart controls to optimize heating systems in existing buildings achieved sustained heating energy savings of 20-40% without major structural works. Systems made buildings ready for low-temperature heat sources while supporting EU Energy Efficiency Directive objectives.

Official source: WE Data Europe ↗

Paris Smart Lighting

France - City of Paris / Eiffage Cielis

Paris upgraded public lighting with connected luminaires and integrated sensors, using monitoring data to intelligently dim lights and cut unnecessary consumption. The network also hosts environmental sensors producing heat maps of air quality and temperature that guide targeted actions, improving quality of life while reducing operating costs.

Official source: Paris lighting project ↗

UK Commercial Property Portfolio

South West England

Multi-utility sub-metering system covering electricity, gas, oil-fired heating, and water across office buildings achieved over five years: 50% reduction in carbon emissions, £50,000 annual energy savings, and 54% reduction in water use (£2,500/year) at one office complex.

Official source: Elcomponent case study ↗
