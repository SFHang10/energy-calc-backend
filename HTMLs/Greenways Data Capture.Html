Section 02 — Hardware
Monitoring Methods

From a simple £15 smart plug to a full IoT sensor network — choose the approach that fits your building, budget, and ambition.

🔌

Smart Plugs

Plug-and-play devices that sit between any appliance and the wall socket. Zero installation expertise needed. Instant cloud logging and mobile app access from day one.

Easiest · £15–30
📡

IoT Sensor Networks

Wireless temperature, pressure, flow, vibration, and level sensors attached directly to equipment. Ideal for commercial kitchens, plant rooms, and refrigerated storage.

Intermediate · £200–800
🏠

Whole-Home Monitors

Advanced · £200–300

Cutting Costs with Data

Energy waste is often invisible — until you start measuring. Monitoring exposes the hidden inefficiencies that silently inflate your electricity bill every single month.

Target range: 40–60%
Warning threshold: 80%+

Normal vs Problem Patterns

Monitoring compressor behaviour reveals equipment health at a glance.

✅
Normal: Compressor runs 150–200W for 20–30 min, then rests for 30–40 min.
⚠️
Concern: Compressor running 80%+ of the time may indicate a failing door seal or thermostat fault.
⚠️
Concern: Very short cycles (under 10 min) suggest overcooling or thermostat issues.
⚠️
Concern: Gradually increasing runtime over weeks indicates declining efficiency — act before failure.
