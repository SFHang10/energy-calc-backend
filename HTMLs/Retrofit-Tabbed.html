Condensing Economiser

Boiler Flue Heat Recovery Retrofit

A condensing economiser recovers waste heat from boiler flue gases that would otherwise be lost to atmosphere, using it to pre-heat boiler feedwater. This reduction in fuel demand increases overall boiler efficiency by capturing both sensible and latent heat through condensation of water vapour in the exhaust gases.

How It Works

  1. Install economiser downstream of the boiler's flue outlet to capture exhaust heat
  2. Route flue gases through a heat exchanger where latent heat (from condensing water vapor) and sensible heat is captured
  3. Preheat incoming cold feedwater before it enters the boiler, meaning the boiler needs less fuel to reach operating temperature
  4. Condensate (acidic water) is drained safely away while clean, cooler gases exit to atmosphere

Retrofit Burner Control System

Precision Combustion Management

Modern microprocessor-based burner control optimizes combustion air/fuel ratio and boiler firing patterns. Mechanical adjustments are replaced with precise electronic controls that match heat output more closely to real demand, reducing excess air and improving combustion efficiency. The result is consistent, optimal combustion across all load conditions.

How It Works

  1. Microprocessor continuously monitors combustion conditions including O‚ÇÇ levels, temperature, and pressure
  2. Electronic controls adjust air/fuel ratio in real-time for optimal stoichiometric combustion
  3. System modulates firing rate to match actual heat demand, eliminating cycling losses
  4. Reduces excess air (typically from 30% down to 10-15%) and improves combustion completeness
  5. Advanced diagnostics alert operators to maintenance needs before efficiency losses occur

HVAC Building Controls

Demand-Driven Ventilation Systems

Smart control systems adjust HVAC fans, extractors, and ventilation based on real-time occupancy, temperature, CO‚ÇÇ levels, and air quality sensors. This demand-responsive approach eliminates wasted energy from over-ventilation during low-occupancy periods while maintaining optimal indoor air quality when the building is in use.

How It Works

  1. Deploy occupancy, CO‚ÇÇ, temperature, and air quality sensors throughout the building zones
  2. Smart controllers receive real-time data and adjust fan speeds and damper positions accordingly
  3. Variable Speed Drives (VSDs) modulate fan motor speed rather than using on/off control
  4. System reduces ventilation during low-occupancy periods and increases during peak use
  5. Continuous optimization based on actual building conditions versus fixed schedules

Refrigeration Cabinet Doors

Strip Curtains & Door Retrofit

Retrofitting display cabinets with doors, strip curtains, or covers reduces ambient warm air infiltration into refrigerated spaces. This simple intervention dramatically cuts refrigeration load and compressor run time while maintaining product visibility and customer access. One of the fastest payback retrofits available.

How It Works

  1. Install glass doors or strip curtains on open-front refrigerated display cases
  2. Physical barrier prevents warm ambient air from mixing with cold refrigerated air
  3. Reduced heat load means refrigeration compressor runs less frequently
  4. Product temperature stability improves, extending shelf life and reducing waste
  5. Customer comfort improves as cold air no longer spills into the aisle

LED Lighting Retrofit Kits

Building Lighting Upgrade

Retrofitting existing fluorescent tubes with LED replacement lamps or retrofit kits that fit into existing fixtures. LED technology consumes up to 50% less energy than fluorescent lighting while offering superior light levels, instant-on performance, longer lifespan (15-25x), and dramatically reduced maintenance costs.

How It Works

  1. Remove existing fluorescent tubes and ballasts from fixtures
  2. Install LED retrofit kits or LED tube replacements designed to fit existing fixtures
  3. LED technology provides instant full brightness without warm-up time
  4. Dramatic reduction in energy consumption and heat generation
  5. 15-25x longer lifespan reduces maintenance and replacement costs
  6. Optional: Add occupancy sensors and daylight dimming for additional savings
