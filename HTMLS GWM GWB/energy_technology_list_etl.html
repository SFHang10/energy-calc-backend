Programme facts below cite the UK ETL or named organisations. We do not quote installation costs, payback periods, or annual savings on this page unless a case study publishes them.

The Energy Technology List (ETL) is one of the world's largest databases of energy-saving technology, featuring thousands of independently verified products across published technology categories, managed by the UK Department for Energy Security and Net Zero (DESNZ). Listed equipment must meet top-quartile energy performance for its category. While UK-based, ETL-listed products are widely used across Europe.

Technology Categories Available

Boiler equipment

High-efficiency boilers and boiler retrofit equipment meeting published ETL performance tests.

Heat pumps

Air-to-water, ground source and water source heat pumps in the top quartile for efficiency.

Refrigeration equipment

Commercial refrigeration cabinets, cold rooms and blast chillers listed on the register.

Heating, ventilation and air-conditioning (HVAC) equipment

Includes efficient white lighting modules, chillers, air handling units and related HVAC products.

Professional foodservice equipment (PFSE)

Combi ovens, induction hobs, hood-type dishwashers and other commercial kitchen equipment.

Heat recovery ventilation units

Mechanical ventilation with heat recovery — listed separately from general HVAC equipment.

Motors, drives & fans

High-efficiency motors, variable-speed drives and fan systems for industrial and building services.

Energy monitoring

Building energy management systems, sub-metering and automatic monitoring equipment.

European Impact

While the ETL is UK-managed, many listed products are manufactured by European companies and meet or exceed EU energy efficiency standards. These technologies support the European Green Deal objectives and are widely adopted across European markets, contributing to continental Net Zero targets and energy security goals.

What ETL listing means for equipment

ETL listing confirms that a product meets published performance tests and category criteria — not a guaranteed saving at your site. Use the register to shortlist efficient options, then model running costs for your own premises.

High-efficiency motors

Listed motors meet ETL energy performance requirements for their technology group — compare nameplate data and category guidance on the register.

Lighting & controls

ETL-listed lighting and control products are assessed against published efficiency criteria for commercial and industrial use.

Variable speed drives

Drives on the ETL meet category performance thresholds; suitability still depends on your load profile and controls strategy.

Refrigeration & HVAC

Professional cooling and ventilation equipment is listed by technology type with verified performance data — not generic payback figures.

Retrofit & replacement

Motor upgrades

Swapping to an ETL-listed motor can improve efficiency where the incumbent unit is below current category thresholds — verify against your duty cycle on the register.

Controls retrofit

Adding listed controls to existing plant is a common upgrade path; savings depend on how the building is operated, not the badge alone.

LED & listed luminaires

ETL-listed lighting meets published efficacy criteria. Compare product sheets for your hours of use rather than assuming a fixed annual saving.

Power quality equipment

Listed power-factor and UPS technologies are assessed on technical performance; commercial impact varies by tariff and load.

ETL Success Stories: Real-World Impact

Named organisations using ETL criteria in procurement — qualitative outcomes only; no cost figures unless published by the organisation.

🚇 Transport for London
Transport for London (TfL) uses ETL criteria as part of their procurement strategy to ensure all electrical and mechanical equipment across the London Underground network meets the highest energy efficiency standards. This approach helps TfL manage operational costs while supporting London's ambitious climate targets.
Result: Standardized energy-efficient procurement across the world's largest metro system
🎓 University of Reading
The University of Reading actively uses the ETL when procuring energy-efficient equipment including high-efficiency hand dryers, boiler systems, and refrigeration units. This systematic approach helps the university reduce operational costs while progressing toward their carbon neutrality goals.
Result: Systematic energy-efficient procurement supporting carbon neutrality targets
🍽️ Foodservice Industry Partnership
The Foodservice Equipment Association (FEA) works closely with the ETL to help businesses identify energy-saving kitchen equipment. This collaboration supports the foodservice sector in making smarter choices that reduce both energy consumption and operating costs during challenging economic times.
Result: Industry-wide adoption of energy-efficient kitchen equipment standards
