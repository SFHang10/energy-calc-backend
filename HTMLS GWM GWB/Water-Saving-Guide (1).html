🌍 Why Water Saving Matters

Water efficiency is one of the most impactful — and most overlooked — sustainability actions available to homes, offices, and restaurants today. The European Environment Agency highlights water efficiency as a key pillar of a water-resilient Europe, as increasing droughts and competing demands put pressure on finite freshwater supplies. Saving water also means saving energy: hot water preparation and pumping are major energy loads, so less water = lower energy bills and fewer carbon emissions.
🌡️

Climate Pressure on Water

Increasing drought frequency across Europe is putting freshwater under strain. Some regions already face seasonal water scarcity despite relatively high average resources per capita.

💶

Rising Water Bills

Water and sewage costs continue to climb across the EU. A typical household or business can save hundreds to thousands of euros annually with the right measures in place.

⚡

Energy Savings Too

Hot water heating accounts for 15–20% of energy bills in many homes and up to 30% in commercial kitchens. Saving water directly reduces this energy load and your carbon footprint.

📜

EU Regulations & Incentives

EU directives and national building codes increasingly require water efficiency standards. Governments offer grants and subsidies to accelerate adoption — making upgrades more affordable than ever.

🚰 5 Proven Ways to Save Water

1
🚰
Tap Aerators & Low-Flow Fixtures
Reduces tap flow from 15 L/min to 5–7 L/min
▼

What It Is

  • Small attachments that fit onto existing taps
  • Mix air with water to maintain perceived pressure
  • No drop in user comfort or performance
  • Available for kitchens, bathrooms, washrooms

Savings

  • Up to 50% less water from faucets at home
  • 40–60% savings in restaurant and office sinks
  • A standard tap: 10–15 L/min reduced to 5–7 L/min
  • Very low cost, typically €5–20 per aerator
Save up to 50% per tap

Best For

  • All household kitchens and bathrooms
  • Restaurant prep and washroom sinks
  • Office restrooms and kitchen areas
  • Fastest payback of any water measure
2
📱
Smart Water Meters & Leak Detection Apps
Real-time usage monitoring — saves 15–25%
▼

What It Is

  • Connected meters that track water use in real time
  • Apps send alerts for leaks and unusual patterns
  • Set daily, weekly, and monthly usage goals
  • Wireless — retrofittable in most buildings

Savings

  • Homes: 15–20% by catching unnoticed leaks
  • Businesses: up to 25% in high-use environments
  • Restaurants see biggest gains — constant kitchen use
  • Leaky toilets can waste 750 litres/day undetected
Save 15–25% by detecting waste

Benefits

  • Identifies leaks in pipes, toilets, appliances
  • Encourages water-conscious habits via data
  • Reduces unexpected high bills
  • Often eligible for business efficiency grants
3
🏠
Upgrade to Water-Efficient Appliances
Certified appliances use 30–60% less water
▼

What It Is

  • WaterSense or EU Ecodesign-rated toilets
  • High-efficiency washing machines
  • Commercial-grade efficient dishwashers
  • Pre-rinse spray valves for commercial kitchens

Savings

  • New toilet alone saves 20–30% indoor water use
  • ENERGY STAR dishwashers save 5,600+ litres/year
  • Pre-rinse valves save 26,500+ litres/year in kitchens
  • Modern eco dishwasher cycles use ~10 L vs 60 L older
Save 30–60% on appliance water

Best For

  • Replacing old toilets in homes and offices
  • Restaurant kitchens — biggest ROI here
  • Laundries and hospitality facilities
  • Often eligible for EU equipment grants
4
🔧
Fix Leaks Promptly
A leaky toilet wastes up to 750 litres/day
▼

The Impact

  • A dripping tap wastes 15–20 litres per day
  • A leaking toilet: up to 750 litres per day
  • Most leaks are invisible — silent but costly
  • A running outdoor hose: thousands per day

Savings

  • Homes: 10–15% of total usage eliminated
  • Businesses: potentially thousands of litres/month
  • Audit + fix pays back immediately
  • Smart meters make leak detection automatic
Save 10–15% instantly

What to Check

  • Toilet cistern seals (dye test)
  • Tap washers and O-rings
  • Pipe joints under sinks
  • Outdoor connections and garden hoses
5
⏰
Sensor-Activated & Timed Fixtures
Prevents water running unnecessarily — saves 30–50%
▼

What It Is

  • Sensor taps that only flow when hands are present
  • Timed taps that shut off after set duration
  • Push-button taps for public washrooms
  • Sensor-controlled urinal flush systems

Savings

  • Homes (high use): 10–20% reduction
  • Businesses/restaurants: up to 30–50% on washroom water
  • Eliminates accidental running taps entirely
  • Especially impactful in high-footfall venues
Save 30–50% in washrooms

Best For

  • Restaurant and café customer bathrooms
  • Office and public building restrooms
  • Schools, gyms, and leisure facilities
  • Any venue with high visitor throughput

🎁 Grants & Subsidies

EU & National Funding for Water Efficiency

Governments across Europe offer significant grants, subsidies, and tax incentives to make water-saving upgrades more affordable. Some schemes cover up to 95% of installation costs for eligible businesses.

🇪🇺

EU Renovation Wave

The EU's Renovation Wave programme allocates €72 billion through 2030 to improve building energy and resource efficiency. Water efficiency upgrades often qualify as part of broader building retrofits.

€72B Available
🇪🇸

Canary Islands — ERDF Tourism Grants

An ERDF-financed programme for non-accommodation tourism SMEs including restaurants has offered grants covering up to 95% of investment costs for energy and resource efficiency upgrades including kitchen equipment.

Up to 95% covered
🇳🇱

Netherlands — ISDE Subsidy

The Dutch Investment Subsidy Sustainable Energy (ISDE) supports businesses and homeowners in installing sustainable technologies. Water-efficient systems combined with energy upgrades often qualify for combined grants.

Combined grants available
🇫🇷

France — MaPrimeRénov'

France's flagship renovation subsidy covers a wide range of building efficiency improvements. Water-saving measures as part of a comprehensive renovation package qualify, with amounts based on household income and measure type.

Income-based grants
🇩🇪

Germany — BEG Programme

The Bundesförderung für effiziente Gebäude (BEG) provides loans and grants for efficient building upgrades. Water efficiency features as part of holistic building refurbishment projects are eligible for inclusion.

Loans + grants
🍽️

EU Single Market Programme — Food

This EU-level programme supports hospitality and food-service stakeholders to implement resource-efficiency practices, including measurement systems and operational improvements that complement technical water savings.

Hospitality focused

💡 Key Lessons from European Practice

1

Integrate Water & Energy

Many successful projects treat water and energy efficiency together, since hot water and pumping are major energy loads. Combined applications often attract higher grants.

2

Target High-Use Sectors First

Hospitality, large offices, and multi-family housing show the strongest returns because fixture use is intensive and savings scale accordingly.

3

Use Subsidies to Unlock Action

High subsidy rates in tourism and SME schemes show that grants can overcome upfront-cost barriers and dramatically accelerate adoption of efficient equipment.

4

Combine Technology & Policy

Studies show that combining efficient fixtures and smart controls with supportive grants and building codes delivers the largest, most cost-effective reductions in water use.

🍽️ Water Saving for Restaurants

Commercial Kitchens & Hospitality

Restaurants are among the highest water-consuming businesses per square metre. Dishwashing, pre-rinsing, cooking, and cleaning all drive intensive use — making them ideal candidates for rapid-payback water saving measures.

🚿

Low-Flow Pre-Rinse Spray Valves

The single highest-impact device for commercial kitchens. Replacing old valves (3–7 gpm) with efficient models (≤1.2–1.6 gpm) can save over 26,500 litres per year, plus the energy cost of heating that water. Payback is typically just a few months.

Save 26,500 L/yr
🍽️

High-Efficiency Commercial Dishwashers

ENERGY STAR-certified commercial dishwashers can save over 21,000 litres of water and ~€300 in energy costs per year. Modern EU Ecodesign models use ~10 litres per cycle vs 60 litres in older machines.

Save 21,000 L/yr
🚰

Faucet Aerators on All Kitchen Taps

Adding aerators to prep sinks, hand-washing stations, and customer washroom taps cuts flow by ~30% while maintaining rinsing performance. Very low cost with fast payback across multiple sink locations.

Save 30% per tap
🚽

Efficient Toilets & Waterless Urinals

High-efficiency or dual-flush toilets and waterless or ultra-low-flow urinals in customer and staff restrooms reduce water use significantly — especially impactful in high-footfall restaurants open long hours.

Save 20–30%
📊

Smart Meters & Leak Detection

Real-time water monitoring catches hidden leaks in toilets, dishwashers, and pipes that can waste thousands of litres silently. Wireless sub-meters can be retrofitted and are widely used in commercial kitchens.

Save up to 25%
⏰

Sensor Taps & Timed Controls

Sensor-activated taps in customer washrooms prevent water running between uses. Combined with eco-mode settings on dishwashers and rack-full sensors, operational savings compound the hardware benefits.

Save 30–50% washroom

💡 Getting the Best ROI — Practical Tips

  • Start with pre-rinse spray valves — fastest payback, often subsidised or free via utility programmes
  • Add aerators to all taps — very low cost, immediate savings, no operational change needed
  • Install a smart meter or sub-meter — reveals hidden leaks and validates other savings
  • Brief staff on scraping plates before rinsing and turning off spray between racks
  • Upgrade dishwashers and restroom fixtures when replacing — choose highest-rated efficiency
  • Consider local water rates and sewer charges — these vary widely and affect payback
  • Check for EU ERDF grants, especially if you're in a tourism or hospitality zone
