Energy savings trajectory

The trajectory view turns your site model, tariffs, and story buckets into a narrative: where energy goes today, what “good” looks like, and which interventions move the needle on kWh and €. It is the bridge between raw feeds and decisions your ops team can act on.

  • Hybrid site totals and live-style pulses show momentum without hiding uncertainty.
  • Toolkit links jump you to intelligence, utilities, or schemes with context carried in the URL.
  • Use it in huddles: pick a resource, read the trajectory strip, then assign an owner from the savings list.

Restaurant equipment deep dive

Deep dives consolidate benchmarks, operating tips, replacement economics, and grant hooks for a class of equipment (for example wok lines or refrigeration). They answer “why does this asset matter?” and “what would better look like on my site?”

  • Curated copy and structure so non-energy specialists still get a clear upgrade path.
  • Ties to marketplace and intelligence when product IDs or names are known.
  • Ideal before capex conversations: finance sees savings language, ops sees behaviour and maintenance cues.

Equipment baseline — why it matters

Baselines anchor “normal” for each asset class on your site. Without them, alerts and savings claims float in mid-air. The dashboard compares live draw, duty cycles, and cost bands to that reference so drift shows up early.

  • Story buckets and KPI strips in trajectory inherit the same baseline language as equipment tiles.
  • When measured use persistently sits above the class curve, treat it as a service or controls review—not only a tariff problem.
  • Document baseline revisions (the audit trail pattern) so retrofits and recommissioning events are visible in history.

Savings projections

When you compare equipment on a deep dive or start a new project, look for the Savings projection control on marketplace alternatives. It opens a payback chart: running cost if you do nothing versus after upgrade, grants and tax offsets, and when cumulative savings cover net purchase price.

  • Real numbers come from the product you pick in context—not from this tour page.
  • Adjust purchase price, grants, and illustrative tax in the popup to stress-test ROI before you commit.
  • Whole-building projections use the same tool from Restaurant Data when you model site-level savings.

Press Savings projection when you see it on a product card to view savings & ROI for that choice. The popup example uses a sample undercounter fridge only—your project picks the real product and numbers.

Equipment intelligence

Measure any monitored asset against its manufacturer or class baseline. Assets sitting systematically above expected kW for the workload often indicate wear, control drift, failed economisers, or fouling—each of which can inflate bills before a hard failure.

  • Efficiency scores in the equipment tab share the same scoring logic for consistency.
  • Use intelligence before you replace hardware: sometimes recommissioning recovers most of the gap.
  • Deep links preserve equipment names so the intelligence page opens focused.
