💧 Water & Energy Savings

Water Saving Devices Reduce Energy Costs Too

Hot water is a hidden energy cost. Reducing how much water you heat directly reduces gas and electricity bills — especially powerful when prices are high.

🚿

Low-Flow Showers & Taps

Reduce hot water demand by 30–50% in homes and restaurants. Lower water heated = lower bills.

🚽

Dual-Flush Toilets

Cut water consumption significantly. Offices and restaurants with high footfall see rapid payback.

🍽️

Pre-Rinse Spray Valves

Commercial kitchen requirement that cuts hot water use by up to 40% versus standard fittings.

☀️

Solar Thermal Water Heating

Heats water directly from the sun, reducing hot water fuel use by 30–50% annually.

✅ Action Plan

How to Protect Yourself From Price Volatility

The earlier you act, the more protected you are when the next price spike arrives. Here's a practical framework for any premises type.

01

Monitor Energy Prices Regularly

Subscribe to Ofgem updates and Eurostat energy data. Knowing when prices are rising helps you time investments and spot the best moments to act.

02

Calculate Your Actual Consumption

Sub-meter key areas (kitchen, lighting, HVAC). You can't manage what you don't measure. Even simple smart plugs reveal surprising usage patterns.

03

Replace Appliances with ETL Products

When appliances need replacing, choose from the UK Energy Technology List. Top-quartile efficiency products cut your kWh demand permanently.

04

Install Solar PV and Batteries

Generate your own electricity during daylight hours. Pair with a battery for evening coverage. A 4 kWp home system can cover most daytime needs.

05

Add Water-Saving Devices

Low-flow taps, spray valves and solar thermal heating reduce both water bills and the energy needed to heat that water — a compounding saving.

06

Review and Repeat Annually

Run the calculation every year against the latest prices. As you reduce consumption, your risk exposure falls — and your ROI on upgrades improves.
