How to read this chart: LCOE is the average cost per kWh over a project lifetime — useful for comparing new supply options, not your retail bill. Onshore wind was the lowest-cost new renewable source in 2024 (USD 0.034/kWh global average), followed by solar PV (USD 0.043/kWh) and hydropower (USD 0.057/kWh), per IRENA. Retail tariffs include network, policy, and supplier margins on top of generation costs.

Data sources & methodology

Greenways uses authoritative public data so you can verify figures and cite them in business cases.

  • Primary — IRENA (International Renewable Energy Agency): Renewable Power Generation Costs in 2024 (full report, July 2025) — global weighted-average LCOE, cost-competitiveness vs fossil fuels, and 2024 commissioning statistics.
  • Summary — IRENA press release (22 July 2025): 91% of new renewable projects cheaper than fossil alternatives — headline stats on 2024 capacity and avoided fossil-fuel costs.
  • Historical context — Our World in Data: Levelized cost of energy by technology — long-run LCOE series (IRENA-backed) used to align 2014–2024 chart points.
  • 2025–2026 chart points: illustrative continuation of the 2022–2024 IRENA trend for planning visuals only — replace with official IRENA annual data when published.

Currency: IRENA publishes in USD; this page displays approximate €/kWh equivalents for European readers. For contracts and finance models, use IRENA’s USD figures or your local tariff data.
