Section 2

Restaurants & hospitality use far more energy per square metre

Food service runs cooking, refrigeration, ventilation, and hot water around the clock. A main supply meter shows the total bill — not which line, shift, or asset is driving waste.

Energy intensity

Restaurants often use ~3× more energy per sq ft than average commercial buildings (EIA). Energy can represent 3–7% of revenue in commercial kitchens.

Section 3

Greenways Buildings process — from data to implemented savings

We capture change at premises and equipment level, evaluate against real business context, then implement targeted efficiency improvements — not just dashboards for their own sake.

1 · Collect data

Submeter electricity, gas, and water where it matters — refrigeration banks, cooking circuits, HVAC, and key assets. Clamp-on options reduce kitchen downtime.

2 · Evaluate impact

Combine metered intervals with operational data: covers, shifts, maintenance logs. Spot faults early — e.g. fridge coils or abnormal dishwasher runtimes.

3 · Implement change

Targeted retrofits, schedule tuning, equipment upgrades, and grant-backed replacements — measured against a real baseline, not guesses.

Basic — fastest payback

Mains utilities + top contributors (refrigeration group, major cooking circuit). Enough to find obvious waste and set baselines.

Standard — recommended for multi-site

Equipment-level visibility: dishwashers, ice, ventilation, DHW loops. Supports predictive maintenance and site-to-site benchmarking.

Advanced — full operational picture

Granular branch metering, alerts, and KPI dashboards for CSRD-style reporting, green finance, and chain-wide performance management.

Section 5

Why Greenways is rolling out restaurant submetering now

Data shows monitoring is one of the quickest, lowest-disruption ways to start cutting energy — before major capex. Greenways is deploying submetering with restaurant partners because measured baselines make every later upgrade credible.

Non-invasive install

Clamp-on flow and CT meters avoid shutting down kitchens — critical when downtime equals lost covers.

Fault detection

Trend logging catches seals, coils, and idle loads in days — not at the next quarterly service.

Proof for funding

Metered savings support grants, green loans, and equipment replacement decisions with real numbers.

Greenways partners with grid data providers and sensor options for electricity, gas, and water across restaurant buildings in the Netherlands — rolling from pilot sites to wider groups with interest from multi-site operators.
