# How will the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism impact imports in 2026

The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) fully enters its definitive regime on **January 1, 2026**, requiring importers to purchase and surrender certificates for embedded emissions in carbon-intensive goods, fundamentally changing import costs and supply chains.[^1][^3]

## Core impacts on imports from 2026

**Financial liability**: Importers pay a carbon price (pegged to weekly EU ETS auction price, ~€80-100/tCO₂e) on embedded emissions for covered goods. Annual declarations due by **August 31** (extended from May).[^3][^8]

**Expanded scope** (Dec 2025 update):

- **Core sectors** (cement, iron/steel, aluminium, fertilisers, electricity, hydrogen) + **180 new downstream products** (machinery, appliances, washing machines using steel/aluminium).[^1]
- Covers ~50% of ETS emissions when fully implemented by 2034.[^4]

**Key affected trade partners**: China, Russia, Turkey, Ukraine, India (top steel/cement exporters); US exporters face compliance uncertainty on sub-supplier emissions data.[^2][^4]

## Practical import workflow 2026

| **Step** | **Requirement** | **Deadline** [^1][^3] |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Register** | Authorised CBAM declarant via national customs | Pre-2026 |
| **Report emissions** | Actual embedded emissions (direct + indirect); default values for ≤20% | Quarterly + annual (Aug 31) |
| **Buy certificates** | €/tCO₂e = weekly ETS average | Ongoing |
| **Surrender** | Certificates matching declared emissions | Annual (Aug 31) |

## Cost impact examples

| **Product** | **Typical emissions** | **CBAM cost @ €90/t** |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| 1t steel | 1.8 tCO₂e | €162 |
| 1t cement | 0.8 tCO₂e | €72 |
| 1t aluminium | 12 tCO₂e | €1,080 |
| Washing machine (steel/al) | ~0.2 tCO₂e | €18/unit |

**De minimis rule**: Small shipments exempt if annual CBAM liability <€50 firm-wide.[^5]

## Anti-circumvention measures (new 2026)

- Pre-consumer scrap emissions now factored into calculations (previously excluded).[^1]
- **Steel/Metal Action Plan** enforcement prevents melting/re-melting routes to dodge tariffs.[^1]


## Business responses needed

**Immediate (Q1 2026)**: Register as CBAM declarant; audit supply chain emissions data; model landed costs.[^2]
**Non-EU producers**: Decarbonise or lose EU market share; scrap-based production now partially priced.[^1]
**EU importers**: Pass-through pricing or eat margin compression; electricity imports face special scrutiny.[^10]

CBAM creates **permanent carbon price parity** between EU-produced and imported goods, with 2026 as the first full year of financial impact (~3% of EU imports initially).[^6]
