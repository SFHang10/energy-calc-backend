# Top funded circular economy topics in Horizon Europe 2024–25

Horizon Europe’s 2024–25 work programmes do not publish a single “league table” by topic, but circular economy money is concentrated in a few recurring themes and destinations, especially in Clusters 4, 5 and 6 and related partnerships. The strongest focus is on circular use of materials and bio‑resources, circular buildings and construction, and circular industrial systems linked to climate and zero‑pollution goals.[^3][^4][^5]

## Cross‑cutting circular themes

- Circular economy is explicitly listed as a key priority axis in the 2025 Horizon Europe work programme alongside zero pollution and sustainable food systems.[^4][^3]
- Work programme documents highlight “resource value retention options (R‑strategies)” such as refuse, reduce, reuse, repair, refurbish, remanufacture and recycle as guiding principles for calls.[^4]


## Circular bio‑based materials and biorefineries

- The Circular Bio‑based Europe Joint Undertaking (CBE JU) under Horizon Europe is a €2 billion public‑private partnership focused on bio‑based circular innovations and full‑scale biorefineries.[^3]
- Flagship topics include establishing biorefineries that turn bio‑waste into higher‑value bio‑based products, integrating collection of bio‑waste and industrial symbiosis with existing waste systems.[^3]


## Circular industry, processes and raw materials

- Cluster 4 (Digital, Industry and Space) prioritises circularity in energy‑intensive industries through partnerships like Processes4Planet and Clean Steel, funding projects that increase resource efficiency and close material loops.[^5]
- Calls emphasise efficient use and recycling of raw materials, support for circular industrial value chains, and “social circular enterprises” that embed circular practices in business and social‑economy models.[^5]


## Circular buildings and construction

- Building and urban‑renovation topics are a major circular-economy funding focus, often linked to the New European Bauhaus and climate‑neutral cities.[^4][^3]
- Prioritised topics include design for adaptability, re‑use and deconstruction of buildings, BIM and digital twins to support circular renovation, and industrialised deep‑renovation workflows that reduce waste and enable material reuse.[^1][^3][^4]


## Circular economy, communities and governance

- Several calls concentrate on circular governance, business models and social innovation, including “social circular enterprises” and neighbourhood‑level circular solutions.[^5][^4]
- Destination texts stress actions that combine technical solutions with new funding schemes and community‑based models to make circular lifestyles and products affordable and adopted at scale.[^4]
