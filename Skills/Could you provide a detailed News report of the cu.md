# Could you provide a detailed News report of the current news and changes in the sustainability industry in Europe highlighting countries, Netherlands, UK, Spain, Portugal but include others European countries also

Provide updates on what the industry is doing concerning sustainable products, low energy electronic products and schemes in regions and include information on the circular economy and strategies to that are incentivizing people and society to be more sustainable, including retrofitting electronic equipment such as from the ETL list by the UK Government

Europe’s sustainability sector is shifting toward circularity, stricter efficiency standards, and incentives for low‑energy products, with notable activity in the Netherlands, UK, Spain, Portugal and at EU level. Policy is increasingly focused on extending product lifetimes, retrofitting equipment, and accelerating sustainable electronics and plastics recycling.[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5][^6]

## EU‑level direction

- The EU is preparing a **Circular** Economy Act for 2026 to improve the single market for secondary raw materials, with pilot measures already launched to boost plastics recycling and circular investments.[^6]
- The Critical Raw Materials Act and related policies aim to cut virgin raw material use, raise recycling of metals and e‑waste, and reduce waste‑sector emissions across member states.[^4][^1]


## Netherlands

- The Netherlands targets a fully circular economy by 2050 and is running a National Circular Economy Programme 2023‑2030 for priority product groups including electronics, packaging and textiles.[^7][^1]
- Dutch initiatives emphasize high‑tech circularity, such as digital product passports and refurbishment for medical devices at Philips, AI‑enabled sorting of aluminum scrap, and tackling low recycling rates of smartphones and other small electronics.[^8][^1]


## United Kingdom and ETL‑style schemes

- The UK Energy Technology List (ETL) is a government‑backed database of around 8,000 independently verified, high‑efficiency products across more than 60 technology categories, including boiler retrofit kits, lighting, motors, refrigeration, UPS and HVAC.[^3][^9]
- ETL criteria select roughly the top 25% most energy‑efficient products in each class, helping businesses and the public sector retrofit existing systems with low‑energy equipment and reduce running costs while aligning with net‑zero product policy.[^9][^3]


## Spain and Portugal

- Spain’s “España Circular 2030” strategy targets a 30% reduction in national material consumption relative to GDP, 15% less waste generation, and halving per‑capita food waste by 2030, supported by EU‑funded circular projects in textiles and industrial processes.[^4]
- Cross‑border initiatives such as CLIC RECYCLE collect waste hair from salons in Spain, Portugal and the Netherlands and turn it into biodegradable products that clean coastal waters and regenerate soils, showing how circular business models engage local communities.[^4]


## Sustainable and low‑energy electronics

- EU research and innovation projects like Sustronics are redesigning electronics using eco‑design, bio‑based materials, and energy‑efficient manufacturing to enable reusable, compostable or more easily recyclable electronic products.[^5]
- Sector‑focused events such as “Circularity in Electronics” in the Netherlands bring industry, policymakers and researchers together around goals to eliminate electronic waste and develop a resilient, circular electronics industry.[^8]


## Circular strategies and social incentives

- Public procurement guidance in Europe highlights the role of purchasers in specifying longer lifetimes, repairability and take‑back schemes for electronic devices, capturing the “unrecognised value” of discarded equipment.[^2]
- Research programmes in the Netherlands and broader EU focus on how circular product design and labelling influence consumer behaviour, aiming to incentivize repair, reuse, and uptake of certified low‑energy products rather than frequent replacement.[^7][^2]
