# Major EU funding programs for circular economy projects in 2024–2025

Major EU funding for circular-economy projects in 2024–2025 mainly came through Horizon Europe (R\&I grants), LIFE (implementation/demonstration), and Cohesion Policy funds (ERDF/Cohesion Fund investments), plus several complementary EU instruments.[^1][^2][^3]

## Horizon Europe (2024–2025)

- **Horizon Europe** funded circular-economy R\&I through multiple “clusters,” most visibly Cluster 4 (Digital, Industry and Space) and Cluster 6 (Food, Bioeconomy, Natural Resources, Agriculture and Environment), with circular economy explicitly embedded in the 2025 Work Programmes.[^4]
- Funding opportunities and open calls for circular-economy topics were (and are) published and managed through the EU **Funding \& Tenders Portal**.[^5]
- The Commission also maintains a dedicated circular-economy R\&I page that links to funding opportunities, projects, and results under Horizon Europe and related instruments.[^2]


## LIFE Programme (2024–2025)

- The **LIFE** Programme includes the sub‑programme “Circular Economy and Quality of Life,” which supports projects on sustainable products, repair/reuse/recycling, and circular business models.[^6]
- Under the LIFE 2024 call outcomes announced in 2025, 132 new LIFE projects were awarded €358 million in EU funding, including **31 projects** supporting “circular economy and quality of life.”[^7]
- LIFE calls (including the 2025 calls) are published by CINEA and routed via the EU Funding \& Tenders Portal for full call documentation and submission.[^5][^1]


## Cohesion Policy funds (ERDF + Cohesion Fund)

- Circular economy is a specific objective under ERDF/Cohesion Fund programming for 2021–2027 (“A Greener Europe”), and a 2024 DG REGIO/JASPERS deck notes **€8.6 billion allocated** to circular-economy investments under Cohesion Policy.[^3]
- This stream typically funds “real economy” capacity like separate collection systems, reuse/repair networks, recycling infrastructure, SME circularity support, and awareness campaigns (depending on the operational programme and region).[^3]


## Other EU channels often used (2024–2025)

- Many circular-economy project opportunities are also bundled into EU-managed calls across programmes visible in one place via the **Funding \& Tenders Portal**, which acts as the central entry point beyond Horizon Europe and LIFE (e.g., cross-sectoral calls run by EU agencies).[^5]
