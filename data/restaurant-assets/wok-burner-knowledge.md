# Wok & commercial burner knowledge (Greenways / deep dive)

Concise reference for agents and maintainers. Facts below are summaries from public articles—verify critical specs with manufacturers.

## Why it matters for the energy dashboard

- Gas wok lines often dominate **peak gas demand** and drive **ventilation load**; induction shifts electrical load but can reduce total energy when accounting for efficiency and hood demand.
- **Idle flame** and **oversized burner vs pan** show up as wasted kWh (gas) or sustained electrical draw (induction with poor discipline).
- Metering **by service period** (rush vs prep) helps separate discipline issues from equipment issues.

## Synthesised technical snapshots

- **Gas wok burners (typical teaching band):** often cited **~30–35%** of fuel energy to useful cooking heat (much loss to room / hood)—optimisation and alternatives target this gap.
- **Induction wok:** direct heating of ferrous cookware; vendor material often cites **~90%+** electrical utilisation at the cooktop—still validate **tariff, duty cycle, and cookware**.
- **Burner categories (commercial jargon):** low-pressure vs high-pressure (wide BTU ranges), stockpot burners, full **wok ranges** (multi-burner lines), **waterless** designs (reduce water-cooling energy waste—vendor-specific).
- **Wok geometry:** round-bottom traditionally pairs with dedicated wok burners; flat-bottom suits more surfaces including many induction hobs (compatibility rules apply).

## Operational levers (non-exhaustive)

- Shut or idle-down between orders; match burner power to pan size; clean jets / maintain flame quality.
- After fuel-switch (e.g. gas → induction), revisit **hood airflow** and make-up air—secondary savings potential.
