# How to Find Service Accounts in MongoDB Atlas

**Current Location:** You're on "All Projects" page  
**Target Location:** Organization Settings ‚Üí Service Accounts

---

## üéØ **Step-by-Step Navigation**

### **Step 1: Go to Organization Settings**

From where you are now:

1. **Look at the LEFT navigation panel**
2. **Scroll down to the bottom** of the left panel
3. **Find "Organization Settings"** (it's at the very bottom)
4. **Click on "Organization Settings"**

**OR**

1. **Click on "ORGANIZATION Greenways"** at the top left (next to MongoDB logo)
2. This should open a dropdown or take you to organization view
3. Look for **"Settings"** or **"Organization Settings"**

---

### **Step 2: Find Service Accounts**

Once in Organization Settings:

1. **Look for a section called:**
   - "Service Accounts"
   - "API Keys"
   - "Access Management"
   - "Security"

2. **Click on "Service Accounts"**

---

## üîç **Alternative Ways to Find It**

### **Method 1: Direct URL**
Try going directly to:
```
https://cloud.mongodb.com/v2#/org/serviceAccounts
```

### **Method 2: Through Top Navigation**
1. Click on the **red bell icon** (notifications) at the top right
2. The alert about expired secrets might be there
3. Click on the alert - it might take you directly to service accounts

### **Method 3: Search Function**
1. Look for a **search bar** at the top
2. Type: "service accounts" or "service account"
3. It should show you the link

---

## üìç **What You Should See**

When you find Service Accounts, you should see:

- A list of service accounts
- Each account showing:
  - Name
  - Status (Expired, Active, etc.)
  - Created date
  - Last used date
  - Actions (Rotate Secret, Delete, etc.)

**Look for:**
- An account with **"Expired"** status
- A **red warning** or **alert icon**
- An account that says it needs renewal
